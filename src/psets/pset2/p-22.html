Let `f : A \to B` and `g : B \to C`

a) If `f` and `g` are both one-to-one functions, show that `g \circ f` is one-to-one.
b) If `g \circ f` is onto, show that `g` is onto.
c) If `g \circ f` one-to-one, show that `f` is one-to-one.
d) If `g \circ f` one-to-one and `f` is onto, show that `g` is one-to-one.
e) If `g \circ f` onto and `g` is one-to-one, show that `f` is onto.
