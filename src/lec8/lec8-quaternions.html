The set `M_n(\mathbb{R})` of all `n \times n` matrices with real scalars is not a group, because not every matrix is invertible. However, the set `GL_n(\mathbb{R})` of all nonsingular (invertible), real, `n \times n` matrices is a group known as the general linear group, and its identity element is the `n \times n` identity matrix `I_n`.

One way to construct groups from subsets of `GL_2(\mathbb{C})` is as follows. Consider the matrices:

1 &= \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \quad\quad\quad I = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \\[10pt] J &= \begin{pmatrix} 0 & i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix} \quad\quad\quad K = \begin{pmatrix} i & 0 \\ 0 & -i \end{pmatrix}\\

Then the set \[ Q_8 = \{\, 1, -1, I, -I, J, -J, K, -K \} \] forms a group under matrix multiplication, known as the quaternion group. Note that `Q_8` is non-Abelian since \[ IJ = K \quad \neq \quad -K = JI. \]
