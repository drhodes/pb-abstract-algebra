A symmetry of a geometric figure is a rearrangement of the figure that preserves the arrangement of its sides and vertices as well as its distances and angles. The set of all symmetries of the regular `n`-gon is denoted by `D_n` and is called the dihedral group.

In this example, we explore `D_4`, the group of the eight symmetries of a square.

The symmetries can be described as follows:

  • Identity: \[ \text{id} = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] A & B & C & D \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Rotation by 90° (counterclockwise): \[ \rho_1 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] D & A & B & C \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Rotation by 180°: \[ \rho_2 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] C & D & A & B \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Rotation by 270°: \[ \rho_3 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] B & C & D & A \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Reflection `\sigma_1`: \[ \sigma_1 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] A & D & C & B \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Reflection `\sigma_2`: \[ \sigma_2 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] D & C & B & A \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Reflection `\sigma_3`: \[ \sigma_3 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] C & B & A & D \end{pmatrix} \]
  • Reflection `\sigma_4`: \[ \sigma_4 = \begin{pmatrix} A & B & C & D \\[5pt] B & A & D & C \end{pmatrix} \]

Multiplication of these symmetries (which corresponds to permutation multiplication) is associative. For example:

\[ \rho_1 \sigma_2 = \sigma_3 \] and \[ \sigma_4 \sigma_1 = \rho_3. \]

In this group, the identity is id, `\rho_1^{-1} = \rho_3`, and every other element is its own inverse. Note that `D_4` is non-Abelian.
